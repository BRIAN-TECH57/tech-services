Pro Tips: Protect Your Hardware

Preventing damage is cheaper than a repair. Follow these expert tips to keep your PC running for years:

1. Keep it Cool & Dust-Free

Dust is the #1 silent killer of laptops. Ensure your PC is on a flat, hard surface (not a bed or carpet) to allow fans to breathe. Blow out dust every 6 months.

2. Use a Surge Protector

Power fluctuations in Meru can fry your motherboard. Always use a high-quality surge protector or UPS to handle voltage spikes.

3. Shutdown Properly

Avoid holding the power button to force a shutdown. This can corrupt your Windows OS or damage your Hard Drive (HDD) over time.

4. Battery Health

Don't leave your laptop plugged in 24/7. Let the battery drain to about 20% once a week to keep the cells active and healthy.
